Protein Salad

Method 01

Ingredients

  • 1 cup spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup purple cabbage
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1/3 cup edamame, shelled
  • 1/4 cup beets, cooked and chopped (save time by buying pre-cooked beets)
  • 2 tbsp slivered almonds
  • 1/3 cup cooked lentils (we purchase them pre-cooked)
  • Salad Dressing of Choice

Instructions

  1. Finely chop the spinach leaves and purple cabbage in a food processor
  2. Add to a bowl with grated carrots, edamame, chopped beets, almonds, and lentils.
  3. Dress with salad dressing of your choice. We used Green Goddess Dressing from Trader Joe's Notes
  4. Finely chopping spinach and cabbage in food processor is optional. We find it helps make eating the salad easier.

Ingredients

  • Quinoa*: 1 cup, cooked
  • Chickpeas*: 1 cup, cooked or canned (drained and rinsed)
  • Edamame*: ½ cup, shelled
  • Tofu or paneer*: 100g, cubed and lightly grilled
  • Avocado*: 1, diced
  • Cherry tomatoes*: 1 cup, halved
  • Cucumber*: 1, diced
  • Red bell pepper*: 1, diced
  • Red onion*: ½, finely chopped
  • Baby spinach or mixed greens*: 2 cups
  • Feta cheese*: ¼ cup, crumbled (optional)
  • Sunflower seeds or pumpkin seeds*: 2 tablespoons

Instructions

  1. *Cook the quinoa*: Cook 1 cup of quinoa according to package instructions. Once cooked, let it cool slightly.
  2. *Prepare the tofu/paneer*: Lightly grill or sauté the tofu or paneer cubes until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. *Mix the dressing*: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, honey or maple syrup, salt, pepper, and minced garlic (if using).
  4. *Assemble the salad*: - In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, chickpeas, edamame, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, red bell pepper, red onion, and avocado. - Add the grilled tofu or paneer cubes to the bowl. - Toss in the baby spinach or mixed greens.
  5. *Add the dressing*: Pour the dressing over the salad and toss everything together until well combined.
  6. *Garnish*: Sprinkle the salad with crumbled feta cheese (if using) and sunflower or pumpkin seeds for an extra crunch.
  7. *Serve*: Serve immediately or chill in the fridge for 15-20 minutes to let the flavors meld.

Method 03

Ingredients

  • Green or brown lentils*: 1 cup, cooked
  • Avocado*: 1, diced
  • Cherry tomatoes*: 1 cup, halved
  • Red bell pepper*: 1, diced
  • Cucumber*: 1, diced
  • Red onion*: ½, finely chopped
  • Baby spinach or arugula*: 2 cups
  • Feta cheese*: ¼ cup, crumbled (optional)
  • Hard-boiled eggs*: 2, sliced (optional for extra protein)
  • Walnuts or almonds*: ¼ cup, toasted and chopped

Instructions

  1. *Cook the lentils*: Rinse 1 cup of lentils and cook them in boiling water until tender (about 20-25 minutes). Drain and let them cool.
  2. *Prepare the dressing*: In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, honey, minced garlic, salt, and pepper.
  • *Assemble the salad*: - In a large bowl, combine the cooked lentils, cherry tomatoes, red bell pepper, cucumber, red onion, and baby spinach or arugula. - Gently fold in the diced avocado. - If using, add the crumbled feta cheese and sliced hard-boiled eggs.
  • *Add the dressing*: Pour the dressing over the salad and toss everything together until evenly coated.
  • *Garnish*: Top the salad with toasted and chopped walnuts or almonds for an extra crunch and added protein.
  • *Serve*: Serve immediately or refrigerate for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
